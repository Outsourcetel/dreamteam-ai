## 1. Operational Vision

### 1.1 The BPO Transformation Imperative

Business process outsourcing was built on a labour arbitrage model: deliver services at lower cost by moving work to lower-cost geographies. For three decades, this model worked. It is now under structural pressure from three directions simultaneously.

**Wage inflation** in historically low-cost markets erodes the arbitrage margin every year. **Client expectations** for quality, speed, and consistency have increased faster than labour arbitrage can satisfy them. **AI capability** has reached the point where a significant portion of structured, repeatable BPO work can be performed by Digital Employees at a fraction of the cost — without geography being a factor at all.

The BPO organisations that will win the next decade are not the ones that resist this shift. They are the ones that redesign their delivery model around it — replacing volume labour with governed Digital Employees, redeploying human expertise to judgment-intensive work, and delivering better outcomes to clients at lower cost with higher margin.

DreamTeam is Outsourcetel's operating system for this transformation.

### 1.2 The Hybrid Workforce Model

Outsourcetel does not operate a pure AI model. It operates a **Hybrid Workforce** — Digital Employees and human specialists working together in a governed, orchestrated delivery model.

The principle is not "replace humans with AI." The principle is: **deploy humans where human judgment, relationship, and accountability are irreplaceable; deploy Digital Employees everywhere else.**

Human specialists in a hybrid model are elevated, not diminished. They no longer spend their days answering the same question for the 400th time. They handle exceptions, build client relationships, review complex cases, make judgment calls, and continuously improve the Digital Workforce that serves them.

The client experiences a service that is faster, more consistent, and more available than a purely human team — while Outsourcetel operates it at significantly lower cost with higher governance and auditability than a traditional offshore model.

### 1.3 What Clients Buy

Clients who outsource to Outsourcetel do not buy AI. They do not buy technology. They do not buy headcount. They buy **operational outcomes**:

- Customer queries answered within SLA
- Transactions reviewed and exceptions flagged
- New clients onboarded to specification
- Accounts reconciled on schedule
- Leads qualified and pipeline maintained
- Compliance obligations met and documented

How Outsourcetel delivers those outcomes — whether through human agents, Digital Employees, or a combination — is an operational decision. The client sees the outcome, the SLA, and the governance report. The internal machinery is Outsourcetel's domain.

### 1.4 The Strategic Opportunity

Every operational domain Outsourcetel runs today is a candidate for Digital Workforce augmentation. Some domains will transition faster than others. Some will always require significant human involvement. But the direction is clear: over the next three to five years, the majority of structured, repeatable, rule-based operational work handled by Outsourcetel will be led by Digital Employees.

This is not a cost-reduction exercise. It is a capability expansion. A Digital Workforce can operate 24 hours a day, seven days a week, across multiple client accounts simultaneously, with perfect consistency, full auditability, and no sick days, staff turnover, or ramp-up time. That is a fundamentally different service proposition than traditional BPO — and it commands a different commercial model.

## 2. Operating Model — Three Maturity Stages

### 2.1 Overview

The transition from traditional BPO to hybrid Digital Workforce delivery does not happen overnight. Outsourcetel's operating model evolves through three maturity stages. Each stage has a defined character, governance model, and expected outcome. Movement between stages is governed — it requires meeting exit criteria, not just making a decision.

### 2.2 Stage 1 — Human Assisted by AI

**Character:** Humans lead all delivery. Digital Employees provide research, drafting, summarisation, and data retrieval support. No autonomous client-facing action without human review.

**Human Responsibilities:**
- Own all client-facing communication
- Make all decisions and approve all outputs
- Handle all exceptions and escalations
- Configure and supervise Digital Employees
- Review DE-generated drafts before sending
- Maintain client relationships

**Digital Employee Responsibilities:**
- Draft responses for human review
- Retrieve and summarise relevant information from knowledge bases and connectors
- Prepare case histories and context packs before human interactions
- Log and categorise inbound work
- Flag cases that match escalation criteria
- Generate first-pass reports for human editing

**Governance:**
- All client-facing outputs require human approval
- Digital Employees operate at `supervised` Trust Level
- Daily human review of DE activity logs
- Weekly performance review of DE outputs

**Approval Model:** 100% human approval on all outputs. No autonomous client-facing action.

**Expected Business Outcomes:**
- 20–35% reduction in average handle time per human agent
- Improved consistency of information used in responses
- Reduced new agent ramp-up time (DE provides instant context)
- Early performance data to calibrate DE configurations

**Transition to Stage 2 — Exit Criteria:**
- DE accuracy rate ≥ 90% on assessed outputs for 60 consecutive days
- Human reviewers confirm DE drafts require minimal editing on ≥ 75% of cases
- Zero critical incidents in 60-day period
- At least one Capability type achieves DE-handled rate of ≥ 50% in shadow mode
- Workspace admin formally approves progression

---

### 2.3 Stage 2 — Hybrid Workforce

**Character:** Digital Employees handle defined, well-scoped Capability types autonomously within policy boundaries. Humans manage exceptions, complex cases, client relationships, and continuous improvement. This is the target steady state for most Outsourcetel delivery teams.

**Human Responsibilities:**
- Handle escalations and complex cases the DE cannot resolve
- Manage client relationships and senior stakeholder communication
- Review DE performance weekly and drive Development Plans
- Approve high-risk and irreversible actions
- Make judgment calls on edge cases and policy exceptions
- Lead QBRs and service reviews with clients

**Digital Employee Responsibilities:**
- Handle defined Capability types end-to-end within policy
- Triage inbound work and route complex cases to humans
- Maintain CRM, ticketing, and knowledge records autonomously
- Execute routine workflows without human initiation
- Escalate proactively when confidence or policy thresholds are reached
- Generate performance and SLA reports for human review

**Governance:**
- Approval required only for irreversible or high-risk actions
- DEs operate at `established` or `trusted` Trust Level
- Automated health monitoring with human review on alerts
- Fortnightly performance reviews; monthly Development Plan updates

**Approval Model:** Risk-based approval. Irreversible and high-risk actions require human sign-off. Routine actions within policy boundaries are autonomous.

**Expected Business Outcomes:**
- 50–70% of defined Capability types handled end-to-end by Digital Employees
- 30–50% reduction in human FTE requirement for covered work types
- 24/7 coverage on DE-handled Capabilities without shift premiums
- Measurable improvement in consistency and SLA compliance
- Human agents redeployed to higher-value exception and relationship work

**Transition to Stage 3 — Exit Criteria:**
- DE-handled rate ≥ 75% across all assigned Capabilities for 90 consecutive days
- DE accuracy rate ≥ 95% on assessed outputs
- Escalation rate within target range (not too high — indicates poor calibration; not too low — indicates overconfidence)
- Client CSAT maintained or improved vs Stage 1 baseline
- Zero policy violations in 90-day period
- Compliance officer and client (if contractually required) formally approve progression
- At least two DEs at `trusted` Trust Level

---

### 2.4 Stage 3 — Digital-First Operations

**Character:** Digital Employees lead delivery across the full Capability set. Humans operate as supervisors, exception handlers, relationship managers, and continuous improvement owners. This is the aspirational state for mature, well-governed Capability types.

**Human Responsibilities:**
- Exception handling for cases the DE cannot resolve or chooses not to handle
- Strategic client relationship management and escalation ownership
- Governance oversight: Trust Level reviews, Certification reviews, Development Plan approvals
- Continuous improvement: identifying new Capabilities, expanding Knowledge, tuning policies
- Commercial relationships: contract management, pricing, scope changes
- Regulatory and compliance accountability (humans remain legally accountable)

**Digital Employee Responsibilities:**
- Lead all first-contact and routine work end-to-end
- Manage their own escalation routing based on confidence and policy thresholds
- Generate client-facing reports and SLA summaries for human sign-off
- Proactively identify knowledge gaps and surface Recommendations
- Coordinate within Workforce Teams for multi-step delivery
- Operate at full autonomy within their defined Trust Level and policy boundaries

**Governance:**
- Approval required only for irreversible actions and client-contractual commitments
- DEs operate at `trusted` or `autonomous` Trust Level
- Continuous automated monitoring; human review of exception reports
- Monthly performance reviews; quarterly certification reviews
- Annual full governance audit by compliance officer

**Approval Model:** Minimal human approval. Restricted to irreversible actions, financial commitments above threshold, and client-contractual matters.

**Expected Business Outcomes:**
- 75–90% of operational volume handled end-to-end by Digital Employees
- Human FTE requirement reduced to supervisors, exception handlers, and relationship managers
- 24/7 consistent delivery across all covered Capability types
- Significantly higher client satisfaction through consistency, speed, and availability
- Outsourcetel margin improvement through structural cost reduction

---

### 2.5 Transition Governance

Movement between stages is a **governance event**, not a project milestone. It requires:

| Requirement | Stage 1 → 2 | Stage 2 → 3 |
|---|---|---|
| Performance evidence | 60-day record | 90-day record |
| Accuracy threshold | ≥ 90% | ≥ 95% |
| Incident-free period | 60 days | 90 days |
| Trust Level requirement | At least supervised → established | At least two DEs at trusted |
| Compliance sign-off | Workspace admin | Compliance officer |
| Client notification | Recommended | Required where contractual |
| Rollback plan | Documented | Documented + tested |

Rollback to the previous stage is always an option. It is not a failure — it is a governance tool. If a Capability type degrades after a stage transition, rollback is automatic for that Capability type while the root cause is investigated.

## 3. Client Delivery Model

### 3.1 What Clients See

Clients who outsource operations to Outsourcetel see:

- A named service delivery team with defined SLAs
- Regular performance reporting (weekly, monthly, QBR)
- A single point of contact for escalations (a human Delivery Manager)
- Consistent, fast, measurable outcomes
- Governance reports confirming compliance, accuracy, and quality

Clients do not see:
- Which work is handled by Digital Employees vs human agents
- Internal orchestration, AI model selection, or technology stack
- Agent-level routing, DE confidence scores, or approval queues
- The DreamTeam platform itself (unless Outsourcetel chooses to disclose it)

This is the standard BPO delivery model: Outsourcetel owns and is accountable for the delivery mechanism. The client buys the outcome.

### 3.2 The Service Contract Map

Every Outsourcetel client contract maps to a Digital Workforce configuration:

| Contract Element | Digital Workforce Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Scope of services | Workspace + Capability set |
| SLA commitments | KPI targets on assigned DEs |
| Escalation path | Escalation rules + Handoff configuration |
| Data handling obligations | Control Fabric data policies + Knowledge Scope |
| Reporting obligations | Automated performance reports + human review |
| Compliance requirements | Compliance Operations DE + audit trail |
| Volume commitments | Capacity planning model |
| Pricing model | Workforce Economics model |

When a new client is onboarded, a client Workspace is created in DreamTeam. The client's scope of services becomes the Capability set. SLAs become KPI targets. Compliance obligations become Control Fabric policies. This mapping is performed during the client onboarding process, not after.

### 3.3 Client-Specific vs Shared Workforce

Outsourcetel operates two delivery models:

**Dedicated Workforce:** A set of Digital Employees and human agents configured exclusively for one client. The client's Knowledge, Connectors, and Policies are isolated. Used for enterprise clients with high-volume, compliance-sensitive, or confidential requirements.

**Shared Workforce:** A Workforce Team serving multiple clients of similar type (e.g. a shared Customer Support team for SMB clients). Digital Employees are configured with client-specific Knowledge Scopes and routing rules but share underlying infrastructure. Cost efficiency is higher; customisation is lower.

Tenant isolation guarantees (see `docs/07_Security_and_Governance.md`) ensure that shared workforce operation never allows cross-client data access, regardless of configuration.

### 3.4 Disclosure and Transparency

Outsourcetel's default position is non-disclosure of the internal delivery mechanism — consistent with standard BPO practice. However, enterprise clients may contractually require disclosure of AI usage. In those cases, Outsourcetel discloses:

- That AI-assisted delivery is part of the model
- The governance framework (Control Fabric, approval rules, audit trail)
- Human oversight structure and escalation model
- Compliance certifications applicable to the AI delivery

What is never disclosed: specific model providers, internal orchestration architecture, prompt configurations, or per-DE performance data beyond what is contractually required.

## 4. Functional Workforce Domains

### 4.1 Domain Design Principles

Each functional domain is designed as a self-contained operational unit with:

- A defined set of Digital Employees
- A human governance structure
- A Capability set
- A KPI framework
- An escalation model
- A DE-handled rate target by maturity stage

The metric used throughout is **DE-handled rate**: the percentage of work items in a Capability type completed end-to-end by a Digital Employee without human intervention. This is the operationally meaningful metric — it is measurable, auditable, and directly maps to staffing implications.

## 5. Digital Workforce Teams

### 5.1 Team Design Philosophy

Digital Employees do not operate in isolation. They operate in **Workforce Teams** — purposeful coordination units that collectively deliver an operational domain. The Workforce Engine orchestrates work within a team; humans govern the team as a whole.

Each team has:
- A shared mission aligned to a client or domain
- Primary and backup DE coverage for every Capability
- A designated human Team Lead (Delivery Manager)
- Shared KPIs and a team health dashboard
- A clear escalation path from DE → human within the team

### 5.2 Support Operations Team

**Mission:** Deliver Tier 1 and Tier 2 customer support across all client accounts within SLA.

**Team Members:**
- Support Specialist DE (primary responder) × 2–3 per client account
- Knowledge Curator DE × 1 per account cluster
- QA Reviewer DE × 1 per delivery pod
- Escalation Analyst DE × 1 per delivery pod
- Human Support Team Lead × 1 per 6–8 DEs
- Human Senior Agents × as required for escalation handling

**Collaboration Model:** Inbound queries route to Support Specialist DE. Complex cases trigger Escalation Analyst DE to prepare context and route to human. QA Reviewer DE samples 10–15% of all DE outputs daily. Knowledge Curator DE monitors gap signals and queues article updates for human approval.

### 5.3 Revenue Operations Team

**Mission:** Maintain a high-quality pipeline through prospecting, qualification, outreach, and CRM hygiene.

**Team Members:**
- BDR DE (prospecting and outreach)
- Lead Qualification DE
- CRM Coordinator DE
- Meeting Prep DE
- Human Business Development Manager × 1

**Collaboration Model:** BDR DE identifies prospects and drafts outreach. Lead Qualification DE scores inbound leads. CRM Coordinator DE updates records after every interaction. Meeting Prep DE prepares briefing packs. Human BD Manager approves outreach sequences, handles relationship-stage conversations, and owns commercial negotiation.

### 5.4 Finance Operations Team

**Mission:** Process financial operations accurately and on schedule, with full audit trail and human sign-off on material transactions.

**Team Members:**
- Billing DE
- AR DE
- Reconciliation DE
- Collections DE
- Human Finance Manager × 1

**Collaboration Model:** Billing DE generates and sends invoices within approved thresholds. AR DE monitors ageing, sends reminders, and flags overdue accounts. Reconciliation DE performs daily reconciliations and surfaces exceptions. Collections DE manages structured collections outreach up to a defined value threshold. Human Finance Manager approves anything above threshold and owns supplier and client financial relationships.

### 5.5 Customer Success Team

**Mission:** Drive client retention and expansion through proactive health monitoring, risk identification, and renewal management.

**Team Members:**
- Customer Success DE (health monitoring and outreach)
- Risk Monitor DE
- QBR Preparation DE
- Renewal Prep DE
- Human Customer Success Manager × 1 per 8–12 client accounts

**Collaboration Model:** Customer Success DE monitors all assigned accounts continuously. Risk Monitor DE surfaces churn signals and creates Handoff packs. QBR Prep DE assembles data packs 3 weeks before review. Renewal Prep DE initiates renewal workflows 90 days before contract end. Human CSM owns all client conversations, QBR facilitation, and commercial decisions.

### 5.6 Knowledge & Quality Team

**Mission:** Maintain knowledge quality and operational quality standards across all client accounts.

**Team Members:**
- Knowledge Curator DE
- Gap Detector DE
- QA Reviewer DE
- Trend Analyst DE
- Human Knowledge and Quality Manager × 1

**Collaboration Model:** Gap Detector and QA Reviewer DEs generate signals continuously. Knowledge Curator DE drafts articles and queues updates for human approval. Trend Analyst DE produces weekly quality trends report. Human Knowledge and Quality Manager owns the knowledge strategy, approves all content changes, and drives quality improvement initiatives.

### 5.7 Service Delivery Management Team

**Mission:** Track, report, and govern all client service delivery commitments, ensuring SLA compliance and maintaining client trust.

**Team Members:**
- SLA Monitor DE
- Client Reporting DE
- Escalation Tracker DE
- Human Delivery Manager × 1 per client portfolio

**Collaboration Model:** SLA Monitor DE tracks live SLA performance and flags at-risk commitments in real time. Client Reporting DE generates weekly and monthly performance reports from live data. Escalation Tracker DE maintains the log of open client escalations and prepares status updates. Human Delivery Manager reviews reports, presents to clients, and owns all escalation conversations.

## 6. Business Development Workforce

### 6.1 The Digital Revenue Development Model

Outsourcetel's growth depends on a consistent, high-quality pipeline of new client opportunities. Traditional BDR operations are expensive, inconsistent, and slow. A Digital Workforce handles the structured, research-intensive, high-volume work of prospecting and qualification so that human business development professionals focus exclusively on relationship and commercial conversations.

### 6.2 Digital Employees

**BDR DE — Prospecting Specialist**
Identifies target organisations matching Outsourcetel's ICP (Industry, company size, current operational challenges, growth signals). Sources data from integrated prospecting connectors. Generates a ranked prospect list with rationale.

Capabilities: Research Target Account, Match Against ICP Criteria, Generate Prospect Brief, Update CRM Record

**Lead Qualification DE**
Evaluates inbound leads against a structured scoring framework. Reviews available data points (company size, industry, inbound intent signals, CRM history). Generates a qualification score with reasoning and recommended next action.

Capabilities: Score Inbound Lead, Identify Qualification Gap, Recommend Next Action, Route Qualified Lead

**Outreach Specialist DE**
Drafts personalised outreach sequences for human review and approval before sending. Personalisation is based on the prospect brief from BDR DE, recent company news, and identified operational challenges.

Capabilities: Draft Outreach Email, Draft LinkedIn Message, Adapt Sequence for Response, Flag Unresponsive Prospect

**CRM Coordinator DE**
Maintains CRM data hygiene across all prospect and client records. Updates records after every interaction, flags stale or incomplete records, and ensures pipeline data is current for human reporting.

Capabilities: Update CRM Record, Flag Stale Record, Deduplicate Entry, Generate Pipeline Report

**Meeting Preparation DE**
When a prospect agrees to a meeting, the Meeting Prep DE assembles a full briefing pack: company overview, identified pain points, relevant Outsourcetel case studies, suggested talking points, and competitive context.

Capabilities: Research Prospect for Meeting, Assemble Briefing Pack, Identify Talking Points, Summarise CRM History

**Competitive Intelligence DE**
Monitors signals about competitor activity — pricing changes, service launches, client wins and losses, job postings that signal capability build-out. Surfaces weekly competitive intelligence summaries for the BD team.

Capabilities: Monitor Competitor Signal, Summarise Competitive Position, Flag Competitive Threat, Update Competitive Knowledge Base

### 6.3 Human Responsibilities in BD

- ICP definition and refinement
- Outreach sequence approval before sending
- All prospect and client conversations
- Commercial and pricing discussions
- Contract negotiations
- Strategic partner relationships
- DE performance review and Development Plan ownership

### 6.4 BD Workflow

```
ICP Defined (Human)
        ↓
BDR DE: Prospect Research and List Generation
        ↓
Human BD Manager: Reviews and approves prospect list
        ↓
Outreach Specialist DE: Drafts personalised sequences
        ↓
Human BD Manager: Approves and sends outreach
        ↓
Lead Qualification DE: Scores inbound responses and new leads
        ↓
Qualified: Meeting Prep DE assembles briefing pack → Human leads meeting
Unqualified: CRM Coordinator DE updates record → nurture sequence
        ↓
CRM Coordinator DE: Updates all records after meeting
        ↓
Competitive Intelligence DE: Ongoing competitive monitoring
```

| Outreach Personalisation Score | ≥ 4.0/5.0 (human-rated) |

## 9. Support & Customer Operations Workforce

### 9.1 The Full Support Stack

Customer Support and Customer Operations is the highest-volume domain in most BPO operations. It is also the domain with the clearest ROI for Digital Workforce deployment: high repetition, well-defined knowledge requirements, structured escalation paths, and measurable quality signals.

The full support stack at Outsourcetel comprises not just the front-line Support Specialist DE but a coordinated team covering quality, knowledge, training, and escalation management.

### 9.2 Support Workflow Architecture

```
Inbound Query (any Channel)
        ↓
Support Specialist DE: Triages, classifies, attempts resolution
        ↓
Confidence ≥ threshold AND within policy → Response delivered
        ↓
Confidence < threshold OR policy trigger → Escalation Analyst DE:
        Prepares context pack → Routes to human agent with full history
        ↓
QA Reviewer DE: Samples 10–15% of all outputs (DE and human)
        ↓
Knowledge Curator DE: Receives gap signals → Drafts articles → Human approves
        ↓
Training Coach DE: Receives QA findings → Generates coaching briefs → Human delivers
        ↓
Trend Analyst DE: Weekly quality summary → Human Quality Manager reviews → Improvement actions
```

### 9.3 First Contact Resolution Architecture

FCR is the most important operational metric in customer support. The Digital Workforce is designed to maximise FCR through:

1. **Comprehensive Knowledge** — every product, policy, process, and exception scenario documented in the Knowledge Collection
2. **Confident resolution** — DE responds only when confidence exceeds threshold; escalates when uncertain
3. **Context continuity** — if a customer contacts again, the DE has full Conversation Memory
4. **Escalation with context** — when a human must handle, they receive the full case history, DE's analysis, and recommended next steps — they never start cold

### 9.4 QA and Continuous Improvement Loop

Quality Assurance in the Digital Workforce model is continuous, not periodic:

- QA Reviewer DE samples and scores outputs daily
- Scores feed directly into the DE's Performance Profile
- Trends feed into Development Plan recommendations
- Knowledge gaps surface as Knowledge Curator DE actions
- Human QA Manager reviews weekly trend reports and approves interventions

This creates a self-reinforcing improvement cycle: poor outputs generate QA signals, which generate Development actions, which improve future outputs.

## 12. Workforce Planning & Capacity Management

### 12.1 Why Capacity Planning Is Non-Negotiable in BPO

BPO contracts carry SLA obligations with financial consequences. If Outsourcetel cannot handle client volume within SLA, the consequences are contractual penalties, client attrition, and reputational damage. In a traditional staffing model, capacity planning means forecasting headcount. In the Digital Workforce model, capacity planning means forecasting DE capacity and ensuring sufficient coverage at all times.

### 12.2 Capacity Model

Each Digital Employee has a defined `max_concurrent_tasks` — the maximum number of simultaneous Capability runs it will execute. Beyond that, tasks queue. The Workforce Planning model ensures that queuing does not cause SLA breach.

**Capacity Calculation:**
```
required_de_capacity = (peak_volume_per_hour × avg_handle_time_minutes) / 60
de_capacity_per_unit = max_concurrent_tasks × (60 / avg_handle_time_minutes)
required_de_units = ceil(required_de_capacity / de_capacity_per_unit)
```

Capacity is calculated per Capability type, per client account, per time window.

### 12.3 Volume Forecasting

The Workforce Planning DE uses historical volume data from connected systems to forecast expected demand:

- **Short-term forecast:** 24-hour and 7-day volume forecast by Capability type and client
- **Medium-term forecast:** 30-day and 90-day forecast for capacity planning and DE onboarding
- **Spike detection:** Identifies historical seasonal patterns, campaign volumes, and client growth signals

Forecasts are reviewed by the Delivery Manager and used to:
- Pre-scale DE capacity for known volume spikes
- Identify periods where human backup capacity must be arranged
- Inform client discussions about volume commitments

### 12.4 Overflow Handling

When volume exceeds DE capacity:

1. **Queue with alert** (default) — tasks queue; Delivery Manager is alerted immediately
2. **Route to human** — tasks above queue threshold route to available human agents
3. **Cross-team routing** — tasks route to DEs in adjacent teams with relevant Capabilities
4. **Emergency escalation** — Delivery Manager activates contingency staffing for sustained overload

Overflow incidents are logged and feed into the capacity planning review. Recurring overflow on a Capability type triggers a capacity expansion recommendation.

### 12.5 DE Onboarding Lead Time

Unlike human staffing, DE capacity can be scaled significantly faster — but not instantly. Adding a new DE requires Configured → Trained → Tested → Certified lifecycle stages. For standard Workforce Role types, this process takes:

- Using a Marketplace template: 2–5 business days
- Custom-built DE: 2–4 weeks depending on Knowledge complexity

Capacity planning must account for this lead time. A forecasted volume spike in 3 weeks cannot be covered by a DE that hasn't started its lifecycle.

## 14. Human Governance

### 14.1 Where Humans Remain Essential

The Digital Workforce handles volume, consistency, and structure. Humans handle judgment, relationship, accountability, and legal authority. The distinction is not about capability — it is about the nature of the decision and the consequence of error.

### 14.2 Non-Negotiable Human Authority Domains

**Executive and Strategic Decisions**
Business strategy, market positioning, major investment decisions, and organisational direction remain wholly human. A DE can prepare briefing packs and synthesise data, but the decision is human.

**Contract Negotiation and Legal Commitments**
No DE enters into a contract, agrees to commercial terms, or makes a legally binding commitment. All commercial negotiations and contract executions are human-owned.

**Pricing Exceptions and Discount Approval**
Any deviation from standard pricing requires human approval. DEs support the process (preparing the deal case, identifying margin impact) but cannot approve exceptions.

**HR-Sensitive Actions**
Disciplinary action, performance management of human employees, redundancy decisions, and sensitive HR matters are wholly human. A DE may surface data and prepare documentation, but all HR decisions are human.

**High-Risk Financial Approvals**
Payments above defined thresholds, supplier contract commitments, and material financial decisions require human authorisation. The threshold is configured per client and per action type in the Control Fabric.

**Strategic Client Relationships**
C-level client relationships, major escalation resolution, and renewal negotiations at significant contract values are human-led. The DE prepares; the human leads.

**Regulatory and Legal Accountability**
Legal and regulatory accountability cannot be delegated to a Digital Employee. The human officer (Director, Compliance Officer, CFO) remains personally accountable for regulatory compliance, statutory filings, and legal obligations.

**Ethical and Reputational Decisions**
Decisions with significant ethical implications, reputational risk, or novel circumstances that fall outside established policy require human judgment. If a Digital Employee encounters a scenario where no policy applies and the action is material, it must escalate — not decide.

### 14.3 The Escalation Covenant

DreamTeam's escalation model is designed around one principle: **a Digital Employee that escalates when it should is more trustworthy than one that never escalates.** Escalation is not failure. It is the correct behaviour of a well-governed AI.

Outsourcetel's human staff must be equipped and incentivised to receive escalations well — to pick up context packs, understand the DE's reasoning, and make the decision efficiently. An escalation that bounces between humans because no one owns the decision is an operational failure, not a DE failure.

Every escalation path must have a defined owner and a defined response time commitment. Escalations that breach their response time SLA are the human team's performance issue, not the DE's.

## 15. Scalability & Future Platform

### 15.1 Operational Today, Platform Tomorrow

Every operational component Outsourcetel builds with DreamTeam today is being designed to be reusable tomorrow. This is not a future migration — it is a present design discipline. Every Digital Employee, every Capability, every Workflow, every Knowledge Collection, and every Workspace created for Outsourcetel's internal operations is built to the same Blueprint, governed by the same Framework, and structured to the same standards as a future Marketplace asset.

The difference between "internal operational component" and "Marketplace product" is packaging and governance — not architecture.

### 15.2 The Reusability Map

| Operational Component | Future Platform Asset | Packaging Required |
|---|---|---|
| Support Specialist DE (per client) | Support Specialist DE Template | Client-specific Knowledge removed; KB templates added |
| Customer Support Workspace | Customer Support Solution Pack | Default policies, KB templates, Channel config |
| Lead Qualification Capability | Marketplace Capability | Configurable ICP criteria, documented I/O schema |

| Compliance Monitor DE | Compliance Pack | Regulatory framework selectable |

### 15.3 Design Rules for Reusability

Every component built for Outsourcetel's operations must follow these rules to ensure future reusability without architectural redesign:

**Rule 1: No hardcoded client-specific values.** Client names, account IDs, specific pricing thresholds, and individual contact details must be in configuration, not in DE definitions or Capability schemas.

**Rule 2: Knowledge is always a separate, swappable layer.** A DE's Knowledge Scope must be a reference to a Knowledge Collection — never embedded content. When the same DE is reused for a different client, only the Knowledge Collection changes.

**Rule 3: Thresholds and policies are configurable.** Approval thresholds, confidence thresholds, escalation triggers, and value limits must be parameters — not constants. Different clients and different industries need different values.

**Rule 4: Connectors are declared, not embedded.** A DE declares which connector type it requires (e.g. `helpdesk`); it does not embed a Salesforce-specific or Zendesk-specific implementation. The Integration layer handles the specific connector.

**Rule 5: Every Capability has a documented schema.** Input schema, output schema, and approval behavior are documented on every Capability. Undocumented Capabilities cannot be packaged.

**Rule 6: Workflows use Capability references, not implementation details.** A Workflow step calls a Capability by name — not an internal function, not a prompt, not a hardcoded API endpoint.

### 15.4 Industry-Specific Solution Packs

The operational domains Outsourcetel runs today — Customer Support, Finance Ops, BDR, CS, Onboarding, Compliance — map directly to future industry-specific Solution Packs:

**Financial Services BPO Pack** — Compliance Monitor DE + Transaction Review DE + Reconciliation DE + Regulatory Reporting Workflow + GDPR/FCA-compliant Control Fabric defaults

**Revenue Operations Pack** — BDR DE + Lead Qualification DE + CRM Coordinator DE + Pipeline Monitor DE + Salesforce Connector Pack + Standard BD KPI framework

Each Solution Pack is a fully configured, certified Marketplace artifact — installed in minutes, customised to the client's specific context, governed from day one.

### 15.5 The Compounding Advantage

Every client Outsourcetel onboards with the Digital Workforce model creates a reusability dividend:

- Client A's Knowledge becomes a template for Client B in the same industry
- A Capability built for one client's support operation is reused for every future client in the same domain
- A Workflow designed for financial reconciliation works for every client with similar processes — with only threshold and connector configuration changing
- Each new Connector integration is available to every client who uses the same external system

Over time, the cost of deploying Digital Workforce solutions to new clients decreases as the reusable asset library grows. This is the compounding advantage of a platform model over a services model: the second deployment of a Solution Pack costs a fraction of the first.
