# Digital Employee Health and Development

## What it is
Two signals on every employee's profile: a **health** state that summarizes how the employee is doing, and a **development** plan of evidence-grounded things it should work on.

## Why it matters
Together they give you an at-a-glance answer to "is this employee doing well, and what should it improve?" — and both are built only from signals that actually exist for that employee. Nothing is estimated or invented; an honest "not enough data yet" is a real state, not a gap being hidden.

## Health signals
The **health** badge appears on the Roster and at the top of the profile. It's composed from the employee's own resolution, confidence and escalation rates, its recent guardrail activity, and its cost. The states are:

- **Healthy** — performing well.
- **Improving** — an open development item is being worked (this is a positive, active state).
- **Low confidence** — its answers are frequently low-confidence.
- **Degraded** — performance has slipped.
- **High cost** — cost per task is elevated.
- **Incident** — an active incident is open against it.
- **Retired** — no longer working.
- **Not enough data yet** — too little activity to judge, shown honestly.

Where cost data exists, the badge also shows a cost-per-task figure.

## Development plans
The **Development** panel proposes and tracks what an employee should work on. Items come from two places:

- **Detected** — proposed from real 8-week performance data: escalation rate, confidence, error rate, and guardrail-block patterns. Click **Scan for development needs** to refresh these. An employee can get more than one item at once if more than one signal is genuinely real; if nothing crosses an evidence-based threshold, you'll see an honest empty state.
- **Manual** — click **+ Add manually** to describe a development need yourself.

Each item moves through **Proposed → In progress → Completed** (or **Dismissed**), using the **Start**, **Complete**, and **Dismiss** buttons. While any item is open, the employee shows as **Improving**.

## How health and development connect
An open development item is what puts an employee in the **Improving** health state — a deliberate signal that a known issue is actively being addressed, rather than being flagged as simply degraded. Resolving or dismissing the item clears that state.

## Related signals on the profile
Development draws on the same real data you can see elsewhere on the profile:

- **Performance** — resolution, confidence, escalation, and error rates.
- **Skills** — evidence-assessed proficiency across five platform skills, measured from real 30-day evidence (never self-reported; auto-assessment tops out at Advanced, with Expert reserved for a human award).
- **Certifications & Reviews** — quarterly performance reviews whose "below" verdict opens an improvement plan with a written consequence.

## Tips & best practices
- Run **Scan for development needs** periodically — it only surfaces items backed by real thresholds, so anything it flags is worth acting on.
- Treat **Improving** as good news: it means an issue has been named and is being worked, not hidden.
- Use the **Skills** and **Performance** panels alongside development to decide whether an employee is ready for more trust.

## Troubleshooting
- **No development items after scanning** — nothing crossed an evidence-based threshold, and none were added manually. That's an honest clean state.
- **Health shows "Not enough data yet"** — the employee hasn't done enough work to compute a reliable signal. It fills in as activity accrues.
