# Understanding Digital Employees

## What it is
A Digital Employee (DE) is a configured, accountable member of your workforce — it has a name, a role, the knowledge it's allowed to see, the systems it's allowed to touch, and a level of trust it has to earn. You manage your Digital Employees from the **Digital Employees** page (the Roster).

## Why it matters
A DE is not a chatbot and not a loose "AI agent." The difference is machinery, not personality:

- **A chatbot** answers from whatever it was trained on and has no memory of your rules. A DE answers **only** from the knowledge you've given it, cites its sources, and escalates to a human when it isn't sure.
- **A generic agent** does whatever its prompt allows. A DE operates inside a **Control Fabric**: it can only read knowledge scoped to it, can only touch systems it has an explicit grant on, is capped by guardrails it cannot override, and earns wider autonomy only from measured evidence.
- **A DE is accountable.** Every answer, block, and escalation is attributed to it by name and written to an immutable audit trail. It has an owner, a lifecycle stage, an incident record, and a performance history — like a real employee.

This is what lets you delegate real work with confidence: you can see exactly what a DE knows, what it can do, and why it made each decision.

## The Roster overview
Open **Digital Employees** to see the Roster — every DE working for your company today, under the heading **Your Digital Employees**. Each row shows:

- The DE's **persona name** (a human first name like "Jordan") and its **role label** (e.g. "Account Success DE").
- A **status** chip (e.g. *active*).
- A **health** chip when there's enough data (e.g. *Healthy*, *Improving*, *Low confidence*).
- Its **department** and a one-line description.

Click any DE to open its profile — identity, lifecycle, trust dial, performance, and more. Below the roster is **Workforce Teams**, where you define who owns an inbox and who covers when they can't.

## Key things to know
- **Every DE is configured independently.** Data access, playbooks, knowledge, and trust build up the same way for every department — there is no special-cased "support bot." A new DE for Finance is set up with the same controls as one for Support.
- **DEs start locked down.** A newly created DE begins *supervised*, with no data access and no playbooks. You grant those deliberately, one at a time.
- **A DE only answers as itself.** The name, title, and purpose you configure are fed into every answer it gives and stamped on every audit record — it never impersonates a generic assistant.
