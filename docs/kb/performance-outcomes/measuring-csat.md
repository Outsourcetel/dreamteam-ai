# Measuring customer satisfaction (CSAT)

## What it is
CSAT is the direct thumbs-up / thumbs-down rating customers give a Digital Employee's answers. It's collected right in the chat where the answer appears, attributed to the DE that answered, and rolled up on **Performance** and **Outcomes**.

## Why it matters
Resolution and confidence measure whether the DE *thinks* it did well. CSAT measures whether the *customer* agreed. It's the only satisfaction signal on the platform that comes straight from the person being served, so it's the truest quality check you have.

## Before you start
- CSAT is real feedback only — there's no fabricated or "proxy" satisfaction score on the live pages. Until customers actually rate answers, the CSAT panels honestly read *"No ratings submitted yet."*
- Ratings are captured from the **support widget** and the **portal chat**, so a customer-facing answer path needs to be in use.

## Step by step (how a rating is collected)
1. A customer asks a question and the Digital Employee answers in the widget or portal chat.
2. Beneath the answer, two buttons appear: **👍** and **👎**.
3. The customer taps one. The chat immediately acknowledges it — *"Thanks for the feedback!"* for 👍, *"Thanks — we'll improve."* for 👎 — and the buttons lock so the same answer can't be rated twice.
4. The rating is saved against that conversation and the DE that answered it.

## Where to see it
- **Performance → Customer satisfaction (CSAT)**: a per-DE list showing each DE's satisfaction percentage and the number of ratings behind it. The percentage is colour-coded — green at 70%+, amber at 40%+, red below.
- **Outcomes → Delivery**: a **CSAT** column per DE (shown as a percentage with the rating count in brackets), sitting alongside health, resolution, and KPI columns.

## Options & settings
CSAT here is deliberately simple: a single 👍 / 👎 per answer (recorded as +1 or −1), attributed to the answering DE. There's no star scale, comment box, or survey builder — the value is honesty and zero friction for the customer. The percentage shown is the share of positive ratings out of all ratings for that DE.

## Tips & best practices
- Read CSAT **together with its rating count**. "100% from 2 ratings" is noise; "82% from 140 ratings" is signal. Both are shown for exactly this reason.
- A DE with strong resolution but weak CSAT is answering *completely* but not *well* — check tone and clarity (tone can be set in your workspace vocabulary settings).
- A single 👎 with an otherwise healthy DE isn't a crisis; a downward CSAT trend across many ratings is. Pair it with the Performance trend sparkline.

## Troubleshooting
- **CSAT panels say "No ratings submitted yet."** No customer has rated an answer yet. This is expected for a new workspace or one that hasn't put a DE in front of customers — it's an honest empty state, not a broken integration.
- **Ratings aren't attributed to a DE.** Attribution is set when the conversation is created by the answer path. Conversations created before CSAT attribution existed won't carry a DE.
